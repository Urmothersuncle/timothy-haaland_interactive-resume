This or That




Norway unfolded like a dream, breathtaking in its quiet beauty.
In the haze of memory, I wander through moments that shaped me, unsure if they were real or merely echoes of a life once lived.
Draped in my Bunad for Syttende Mai, I walked the streets in celebration of Norway’s heart, the Constitution Day parade.
My mother’s voice soared from the float, carried through the town on waves of song.

I, on the other hand, was not on the float.

Marching behind, whilst feeling every thread of that itchy tradition against my skin.
Snow dusts the village like a quiet breath, wrapping the colorful homes in a blanket of stillness. The mist hangs low, blurring the line between mountain and sky, as if nature itself is softly whispering. This is a peaceful corner of the world, where life moves at its own pace, tucked away from time.
Golden light spills over the peaks, kissing the fjords below. Villages hug the water’s edge, small and timeless, while mountains stand tall like ancient sentinels. Here, the land feels endless, the air thick with quiet, as if the world is pausing to let you breathe in its beauty.
A wooden sign, weathered yet steadfast, points the way to Preikestolen. Carved letters stand bold against the rugged stone, guiding adventurers to one of Norway’s natural wonders. The path ahead promises both challenge and awe, where the land meets the sky in a breathtaking view.
Here, at the edge of Preikestolen, the world feels boundless. The sharp cliff juts out over the serene, teal waters of the fjord below. It is a place where earth and sky seem to merge, where the vastness humbles you. Visitors stand on the brink, dwarfed by nature, each of them embracing the wonder and weight of this view that steals both breath and words.
Here, at the precipice, where awe meets danger, my brother Knowlton sits—boots dangling above the sheer drop. They often warned us not to stand too close to the edge, for the wind and wonder could play tricks on balance, pulling you toward the void. Once, a woman lost her footing here, teetering on the brink of tragedy. In that moment, it was Knowlton who reached out, grounding her in the solidity of the cliff, pulling her back from the grasp of an unwanted fate. It’s moments like these that make us remember just how fragile and fleeting safety can be at the edge of wonder.
As the sun dips beneath the horizon, the three swords rise silently, guardians of the past. Their shadows stretch over the still water, reflecting a history of resilience and strength. In this quiet moment, the sky and earth seem to meet in peaceful reverence, as day fades away.
These wooden cabins stand tall, their intricate carvings and moss-covered roofs whispering stories of generations long past. Nestled in the heart of the forest, they feel as though they’ve grown from the earth itself, rooted in history. Sunlight warms the weathered timber as children wander freely, unaware they walk among echoes of old Norwegian life.
Here we sit, my father, sister, and I, at the base of this wooden cabin, its weathered beams holding stories older than us. The sun warms our backs as we rest on the stones below, a pause in time amidst this piece of history. The carved wood, the grassy field, and the simplicity of the moment remind me that sometimes, it's the quietest scenes that hold the most meaning.
Here I am posing with my oldest brother Knowlton, the giant of my world at the time. His room, a gallery of memories, every inch of the walls covered with photos—snapshots of lives, moments, and adventures that framed his journey. It was like standing in the middle of a story told through images, surrounded by the past as we playfully posed, illustrating our strength and stature.
Time to time our town would glow softly under an endless twilight, the mountains standing tall like silent guardians. I remember nights like this, when I would wander into my parents’ room, confused by the light streaming through the windows, even though the clock read 11pm. It felt as though the world refused to sleep, bathed in a soft, northern glow. Those long summer nights seemed to stretch on forever, a gentle reminder that in this place, time followed its own rhythm, just as I did.
This was our home, standing quietly in the glow of fading light. In that living room, we’d gather and watch as wild reindeer, graceful and uninvited, wandered through the driveway garden, nibbling at the roses. They came as if they owned the place, leaving us in awe of the beauty that surrounded our everyday lives. So many memories bloomed here—just like the roses—small moments that filled the air with laughter, warmth, and the soft hum of life. Each corner of this house holds a piece of us, etched into the walls and garden forever.
This house wasn’t just walls and windows; it was the stage for our wildest adventures. The second-story balcony, while meant for quiet moments, became our launching pad. We’d leap off, flying through the air onto the trampoline below, feeling weightless for a split second before bouncing back up, laughing all the way. Sometimes, we’d shoot basketballs mid-air, challenging ourselves with trick shots only kids could dream up. Sure, there were hospital trips—a few too many, perhaps—but the thrill, the joy, and the laughter were worth every bruise and broken bone. Those memories, made in the heat of youthful courage, were priceless.
This was one of our hytte, one of three, scattered across the Norwegian wilderness. Each visit felt like an escape into the heart of nature, where we belonged just as much as the wildlife we befriended, knowing this was their home too. With no TV or smartphones, we found warmth in the bunk beds stacked high, and spent hours around board games like Yahtzee and Monopoly. The silence outside was filled with the crunch of mountain bike tires on rocky trails or the steady rhythm of our boots as we hiked. One memory, so surreal it's hard to believe it was ever mine, was the freedom to explore completely unsupervised. I once ventured off alone and after a couple hours of hiking, I found a kind family who welcomed me in, fed me lamb chops, and sent me on my way—no panic, no fear. Just a trust that this was what we were made for. That freedom to explore shaped me, and the solitude built a quiet strength I still hold dear.
One funny memory from this hytte was on my birthday, as I was out picking blueberries and strawberries for the cake. I got a call from one of my brothers, telling me it was getting late and dinner was ready. So, I began heading back inside. As I opened the door, I was met with a fierce growl. There, in the middle of our living room, stood a massive grizzly bear. I gasped and jumped, and to this day, I swear my feet spun around like in a cartoon, running before the rest of my body caught up or even hit the ground. Blueberries and strawberries painted the cabin walls from the ceiling to the floor. I then slammed the door and bolted, but unfortunately, I was already far passed the first step on the staircase when gravity finally caught up with me, landing me at the bottom of the stairs. For a moment, I thought it was the end—until I heard hysterical laughter coming from the room with the "bear."

I realized it was a prank. Walking back up, I found my second oldest brother, Christen, pulling the grizzly bear rug off himself, laughing with my other three siblings, Knowlton, Elisa and Amy, while picking up the berries I had scattered in panic.
Surrounded by the quiet majesty of Norway’s mountains and wilderness, It was in the Hyttes that we spent our summers picking berries, hiking the rugged trails, and having memorable encounters—But it wasn't all laughs and pranks. Some memories were of adventure and near mishaps, like when my mom and I were exploring and came upon some cute small structures that resembled houses, then once too close, quickly realized we were in the middle of a Bee farm. We sprinted for our lives, dodging the bees with my mom laughing until she realized one was stuck in her hair. I'm not sure if you've ever struggled with dancing, but some pro advice I learned that day, is that if ever you get a bee stuck in your hair, believe me, dancing come naturally. I had to pick it out while we nervously hoped we wouldn't get stung. Luckily though, we made it back safely, another adventure etched into our memory.

Winters were no less magical. Voss became our snowy playground, where I found my passion for skiing, zipping down the slopes with confidence, collecting competition victories as I went, all while storing bacon from the resorts in my pockets for a quick snack on the slopes. Every corner of this wild, free landscape holds memories that shaped who I am today—whether it was the thrill of skiing or the simple joy of sitting on the porch after a day of mountain biking. Each visit to the hyttes offered something new, another story to tell, another moment of pure freedom in the heart of nature.
London was a whirlwind of red buses, towering clocks, and the hum of the city’s heartbeat.
We rode the London Eye, ate some hotdogs from the markets below, climbed aboard double-decker buses, each ride like a mini adventure through history.
This moment, atop of the Lion, felt as if I was an heir to a forgotten throne, conquering the journey with a smile as wide as the horizon.
From the Eye, the city sprawls beneath us, the heartbeat of London visible in every street, every whispering alley.
Towering over the cityscape, Big Ben stands tall, a timeless guardian of time, its hands a constant rhythm guiding London's pulse.
Beneath the watchful eye of the lion, childhood innocence reigns, as if I had tamed the mighty beast for a fleeting moment.
The Phantom of the Opera filled the air with melodies that lingered long after the curtain closed. The Science Museum’s wonders left us wide-eyed, where knowledge seemed to float as freely as the Thames. These moments became snapshots in time, etched into memory alongside the towering landmarks, making every step in London feel like a page from a storybook.
London wrapped itself around my heart like a cherished secret, and as we prepared to leave, a quiet sadness lingered. The city that had become a favorite felt like home, and each glance back was a soft farewell, knowing it would always be a place I longed to return.
Here we are in Denmark. With sticky fingers and wide smiles, the simplest of joys unfold, marshmallows melting on a stick. Childhood innocence framed by rugged cliffs and an endless sky—moments that sweeten memory.
The warm glow of embers flickers beneath a sky kissed by mountain air. Family laughter mingles with the scent of roasted marshmallows, a quiet joy shared with nature as the world sprawls in peaceful harmony behind us.
The rolling Danish hills and winding roads stretch beneath the blue horizon. Somewhere between the landscapes and laughter, the spirit of adventure whispers, calling us to explore the open spaces.
Behind the wheel, eyes full of focus, I race forward—hands gripping the wheel of childhood. At Legoland, the track was mine, and my imagination sped far beyond.
With cobblestone beneath my feet, I'd chase the pigeons swift and fleet. Those downtown streets, a youthful race, a fleeting dance, a childhood chase. In my heart, those colorful homes still find their grace, a cherished memory time cannot erase.
In laughter, generations meet, A Viking helm, a playful feat, with joy and family, stories blend, memories shared with grins that mend.
Dressed in tradition, flags held high, my sister Amy and I smile beneath the sky, a day to celebrate the land, with heart and joy, hand in hand.
We would dangle our feet above the roar, to observe nature's power, which we adored. The waterfall would cascade below, Its mighty force in endless flow.
In my early years, I was raised along the thundering presence of waterfalls, demanding to be revered.
I miss how through the mountains, they carve rapid paths–untamed beauty in nature’s wrath.
Sadly today though, I fear, the mountains remain loud in my heart but not in my ears.
These chocolates were the poetry of my youth.
Sliding down the rock, I kept my balance steady, knowing the mountains' secret—once you start running, it’s hard to stop. The path may seem playful, but every step demands respect, careful and sure, for the mountain is as unforgiving as it is beautiful.
This house, bathed in evening light, holds the laughter, the quiet, and the years in its embrace. Though I will miss its walls and windows that framed my world, I carry its warmth with me, thankful for the memories it gave. It will forever be the place that shaped me, though I step beyond its doors.
After moving to America, new friendships blossomed like the sun breaking through clouds. Each face, each name, became a thread woven into the fabric of my life. Meeting people from all walks of life, I found the chance to connect, to grow, and to create bonds that time won’t easily fade. This is the first true friend I made, Chuck-A-Duck.
As a testament to embracing change and growth, I embraced my inner Viking, letting my hair flow freely. This was an incredible time in life, where each day promised new adventures and the thrill of the unknown.
This was a time when the stage lights felt like home, and I had the incredible opportunity to sing and travel with amazing people. Each note carried a sense of freedom, and the shared energy with the crowd became an unforgettable rhythm, one that still echoes in my heart.
This was our team, a group bound by music and shared passion. Together we traveled, our voices harmonizing not just on stage but in life’s many adventures. Each journey left its mark, and every performance was a memory crafted with the friends I was lucky enough to sing beside.
While still on the topic of my favorite things, as you can see here, Christmas has always been one of my favorite holidays.
So much so, that I was even selected to be one of the lead roles in the annual Cajun Christmas Production, where I got to sing and dance with an amazing team. It was a joyous time filled with laughter, costumes, and the holiday spirit, making Christmas even more magical.
This was the Christmas crew, a merry band of elves, laughter, and cheer. Together, we brought holiday magic to life.
The grand finale was a burst of joy and color, every elf, every note, and every laugh coming together in perfect harmony. With Chuck-A-Duck as Rudolf, the very heavy reindeer, we lifted the magic even higher, carrying the holiday spirit on our shoulders—literally. The stage erupted in laughter and cheer, as we ended the night on a note as festive as it was unforgettable.
Other than rocking the stages with productions, I was fortunate enough to work with Microsoft, hosting conferences with up to 26,000 attendees. We traveled across the US, sharing in the excitement of innovation and creating unforgettable experiences along the way. A fun memory from this time was how the majority of our team had never flown before and were anxious during turbulence. I’d pull out my ukulele and start playing "Ride" by 21 Pilots. After the mix of laughter and playful boos, song requests would roll in, and I’d play to help ease the nerves. Those impromptu jam sessions in the clouds became a cherished part of the adventure.
One thing I've learned is that everything is temporary, and this photo marks the last day my hair would be long, a symbol of letting go. Change is inevitable, and though moments like these pass, their memories linger—just as this quiet moment did before a new chapter began.
Although the adventures and experiences were always great, what I'm most thankful for is the people I was lucky enough to meet along the way, like my lifelong friend Austin. Through all the highs and lows, it’s the connections that remain constant, grounding me through every chapter.
I've grown up a lot since Norway. Myself and best friends, who I've known now for close to 15 years, have families of their own. In this photo, I'm holding my daughter Aurora, and it’s a blessing to have a family of my own with my partner Erin and my step-daughter Zyla. It's really nice being surrounded by trustworthy people who are always honest with you and there for you.
I'll always miss Norway, where the mountains stood tall watching over me, and the Aurora danced across the sky like magic. It often feels as if a part of me is missing without that natural wonder—an ache for the wild beauty that once surrounded me.
But nothing makes me happier than being surrounded by incredible friends and living life alongside my beautiful family. I may no longer see the Aurora Borealis in the sky, but the Aurora in my life today, my beautiful daughter, is the only light in my eyes that I truly need.
